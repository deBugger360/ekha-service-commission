BRIEF HISTORY OF

The Ekiti State House of Assembly Service Commission

Ekiti State House of Assembly Service Commission is creation of the Ekiti State House of Assembly law NO 4 2003 The Commission arms to achieve a permanent and competent staff system for the legislative arm of government. The bill establishing the Commission was passed on the 27th October, 2003 and was inaugurated in August 2005

Like other constitutional Commissions in the Public Service, the House of Assembly Service Commission is saddled with the responsibilities of appointments, promotion exercising disciplinary control over staff or person holding or acting in each office of the Legislative service in accordance with service rules and regulations, performing other specific functions such as organizing seminars relating each departments and also developing and improving the legislative bureaucrats by providing adequate manpower requirements in the Legislative service

The pioneer Chairman was Hon. (Dr) Odunayo Fagbeyiro 2005‐2008. The Commission at the time had the Chairman as the only one with full time status while other members were `. The second Commission was headed by Hon. Akogun Bunmi Ogunleye, 2008‐2010. In the year 2009, the Commission law had its first amendment, where all members were placed on full time. In October 2010, the third Commission came on board and was first headed by Hon. Bayo Idowu 2010‐Dec 2012. He was later replaced with the late Hon. Chief George Femi Ojo.

Rt. Hon. Tunji Odeyemi became the fourth Commission Chairman in the year 2015, while the Fifth Commission came on board in 2019 under the leadership of Rt. Hon. Talwo Olatunbosun as Chairman and the tenure ended in the year 2022. The Fifth Assembly Service Commission period witnessed the struggle for administrative and financial autonomy for the legislative arm, though administrative autonomy had been in existence before the Fifth Commission which led to a stoppage of postings of staff from the other arm of government to serve in the Ekiti State House of Assembly. This act led to significant growth in the personnel outfit of the legislature.

Since 1997 when the Assembly came on board with about just 9 staff its strength has now grown to about 300 with various departments such as: Legislative, Budget/Research and Planning Information, Women and Child Development, Administrative, ICT Training Procurement among others As part of efforts to expand growth of the legislative arm of government, members of the Commission were increased from Five to seven in the Sixth Commission headed by Hon (Engr) Kayode Fasakin. The daily administration of the Commission is placed in the hands of the Executive Secretary, who serves as the administrative head while the Clerk of the House serves as the head of legislative service.

Vision of The Commission
  • Effective Management of Personnel outfit of the Legislative
  • Providing Supportive Services to the Legislative Arm of Government
